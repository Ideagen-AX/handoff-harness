01 · THE PROCESS
The process & the pain

What it costs us every week.

After every UX change a constellation of people across the company need to be informed. Today, if that communication happens at all, it's done ad-hoc, and fully manually. If the handoff package produced by Blue Book were made by hand — all of the collecting of material, analysis and synthesis, writing and coding and making slides, and sending emails, and Teams messages, and setting up calls, and having those calls — the total time spent is estimated at 104 hours, conservatively.

02 · THE HARNESS
Our harness — an environment, not one automation

Five layers, one harness.

Context

EHSQ-E product & design-system knowledge — modules, personas, tokens, house voice — fetched on demand.

Skills

Build the change brief · draft 9 audience artifacts · generate a DS-grounded starting component.

Connectors

Claude via the Vercel AI Gateway; read-only prototype and codebase.

Guardrails

Nothing sends. Every draft is human-reviewed; the brief flags computed vs inferred.

Adoption & memory

Runs per change in minutes; every run archived to a versioned library.

03 · THE DEMO
Live & end to end

The agent, on real input.

Switch to the live run or your screen recording here — let the agent's decisions carry the story.

  • Fetches the before & after prototypes and diffs them — visually and in code.
  • Writes one change brief, captures the screens, fans out 9 artifacts in parallel.
  • Chooses its tools, the states to screenshot, and the component-impact call — unprompted.
  • Release notes, QA cases, docs, an exec slide, and a coded Vue component — export-ready.

04 · GUARDRAILS
Every capability pairs with a guardrail

Powerful, and governed.

Drafts 9 downstream artifacts→A human edits & approves each — nothing sends
Reads the live prototype & codebase→Read-only and scoped; secrets refused
Infers what changed→Flags computed vs inferred; never claims an unverified diff
Generates component code→Labelled a starting point; linted; a dev reviews before merge

05 · IMPACT
What we stop doing on Monday
104 hours back.
