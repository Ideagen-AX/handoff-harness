# Audience spec — Internal blog / presentation

**Who they are:** Internal audiences — other product teams, execs, adjacent stakeholders.
They want the story: what shipped, why it matters, and what it signals about direction.
This is the "middle-weight" artifact between a terse release note and a full support guide.

**What they care about:**
- The narrative: the problem, the approach, the outcome
- Why this was worth doing (strategic/user value)
- A sense of craft and momentum (this is also internal marketing for the team's work)

**Tone:** Engaging, confident, narrative. Plain-spoken but with some polish. First-person
plural ("we") is fine. Not a spec, not a tweet.

**Format:** Short post — a hook, 2–4 short paragraphs, optional pull-quote or key-points
list. ~200–350 words. Can reference screens/states by name.

**Must include:**
- A hook that frames the problem
- What was built and the key idea behind it
- Why it matters for users / the product
- A forward-looking closing line

**Must avoid:** Dense checklists, raw component inventories, over-claiming, wall of text.

---

**Example (responsive-search):**

> **One search, six ways to see it**
>
> Search is where a lot of EHSQ work starts — and until now, it showed everyone the same
> rigid layout no matter what they were trying to do. If you were scanning hundreds of
> CAPAs, comparing due dates, or briefing a meeting, you got the same fixed table, and on a
> smaller screen it didn't hold together at all.
>
> We rebuilt search results to be genuinely responsive — in two senses. Results now render
> in whichever of six modes fits the task (list, table, cards, calendar, hierarchy, chart),
> and the filter and visualization panels reshape themselves — drawer, modal, docked, or
> full-page — to fit whatever screen you're on. We also added an expression builder so power
> users can chain filters with AND/OR and grouping.
>
> The bigger idea: the same data, presented the way the moment demands. It's a pattern we
> think extends well beyond search.
